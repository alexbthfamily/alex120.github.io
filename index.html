Overview

The 2016 NBA Finals was a best-of-seven series between the Cleveland Cavaliers and the Golden State Warriors. The Cavaliers made history by coming back from a 3-1 deficit to win the championship.

Teams

Cleveland Cavaliers

Led by LeBron James, Kyrie Irving, and Kevin Love.

Golden State Warriors

Defending champions with Stephen Curry, Klay Thompson, and Draymond Green.

Key Players

  • LeBron James: Finals MVP, led the series in points, rebounds, assists, steals, and blocks.
  • Kyrie Irving: Hit the clutch Game 7 three-pointer over Stephen Curry.
  • Stephen Curry: Reigning MVP, but struggled in Game 7.
  • Draymond Green: Triple-double in Game 7 but was suspended in Game 5.

Game Summaries

Game 1: Warriors win 104-89.

Game 2: Warriors win 110-77.

Game 3: Cavaliers win 120-90.

Game 4: Warriors win 108-97.

Game 5: Cavaliers win 112-97.

Game 6: Cavaliers win 115-101.

Game 7: Cavaliers win 93-89, completing the comeback.

Legacy

The 2016 NBA Finals is considered one of the greatest series in NBA history. LeBron James delivered on his promise to bring a championship to Cleveland, and the series cemented his legacy as one of the greatest players of all time.
